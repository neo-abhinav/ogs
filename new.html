Oak Grove School - A Legacy of 138 Years

Oak Grove School, the first Railway hill school, was started in 1870 by the erstwhile Sindh, Punjab and Delhi (SPD) Railway, which later came to be known as North Western Railway. It was established at a bungalow called 'Fairlawn', which was part of the Rajah of Nepal's estate and close to the place where Oak Grove School is now located. The Fairlawn school was merged with Oak Grove School in 1894 as the number of students had increased and there was a dearth of staff to manage the school. Oak Grove School started with only 28 students on its rolls and currently has more than 600 students.

Mission and Vision

The mission of Oak Grove School is to provide a holistic education that nurtures the intellectual, emotional, and social development of each student. The vision is to create a learning environment that fosters creativity, critical thinking, and a sense of responsibility towards society.

Facilities

Oak Grove School offers a range of facilities including well-equipped classrooms, science and computer laboratories, a library, sports facilities, and a dedicated arts and music department. The school aims to provide a balanced education that includes academics, sports, and extracurricular activities.

Achievements

Over the years, Oak Grove School has achieved numerous accolades in academics, sports, and cultural activities. The school has consistently ranked among the top government boarding schools in India and has produced many successful alumni who have excelled in various fields.

Community Engagement

Oak Grove School believes in giving back to the community. The school regularly organizes outreach programs and community service initiatives to instill a sense of social responsibility among students.
